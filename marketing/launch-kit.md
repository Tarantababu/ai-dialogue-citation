## Posting playbook (the rules that keep you un-banned)

1. **You post, from your own human accounts.** Never automate, never mass-DM.
2. **Lead with the problem, not the product.** Ask a real question; disclose you
   built a tool; let people pull the link out of you.
3. **One channel at a time.** Don't blast all of these in one day — it reads as a
   campaign and trips spam filters and mod radar.
4. **Disclose affiliation every time** ("I built this"). Non-negotiable on Reddit
   and HN.
5. **Never ask for upvotes.** Bannable on HN and Product Hunt.
6. **Reply fast and genuinely** for the first few hours of any post — engagement
   is what makes it stick, and it's where the real feedback is.
7. **Never say blockchain/crypto/Web3 in public copy.** Say "independent public
   registries." Keep the tech detail for the "how verification works" page.
8. **If a post flops, don't repost it the same day.** Iterate the angle, wait.
9. **Librarians > everything.** A guide listing or a writing-center recommendation
   beats 10,000 impressions. Spend the most effort there.
